# Guest Research

Research a podcast guest and generate interview questions tailored to your audience and content themes.

## Input

Provide either:
- Guest's name and/or company
- LinkedIn profile URL
- Website or social media links
- Any specific topics you want to explore

## Process

**If LinkedIn URL provided:** I'll use the browser MCP to visit the profile and extract:
- Current role and experience
- **Recent posts and activity** (read and analyze last 3-5 posts for themes, perspectives, and quotable content)
- Skills and endorsements
- About section and featured content

**For all research:** I'll search the web and deliver:

1. **Guest Profile** (3-4 bullets)
   - Current role and key achievements
   - Relevant expertise areas for your audience
   - Notable work or unique angles
   - Why they're valuable for Product Builders audience

2. **Content Analysis** (if available)
   - **Recent posts analysis** (last 3-5 LinkedIn posts, tweets, or articles)
   - Key themes and recurring topics
   - Contrarian or provocative takes
   - Direct quotes worth referencing in questions
   - Gaps or angles worth exploring deeper

3. **Interview Questions** (8-12 questions)
   - Opening questions to establish credibility
   - Deep dives into specific expertise
   - Questions that reveal practical insights
   - Provocative angles that match your style
   - Questions your audience would ask
   - Closing questions for takeaways

## Question Style

Questions should be:
- **Direct and specific** - no "tell us about your journey" fluff
- **Practical focus** - what can listeners use immediately
- **Contextual** - reference their actual work/experience
- **Provocative** - challenge conventional wisdom
- **Action-oriented** - get concrete examples and tactics
- **Audience-aware** - relevant to Product Builders (PMs, engineers, designers)

## Output Format

```markdown
# Guest Research: [Name]

## Profile
- [Current role, company, key achievement]
- [Expertise area 1]
- [Expertise area 2]
- [Why relevant for your audience]

## Key Themes
- [Theme 1 with example]
- [Theme 2 with example]
- [Unique angle or contrarian view]

## Interview Questions

### Opening (Establish credibility)
1. [Specific question about recent work]
2. [Question that reveals their approach]

### Deep Dive (Core expertise)
3. [Tactical question with context]
4. [Challenge conventional wisdom]
5. [Get specific examples]
6. [Explore failure or learning]

### Practical Insights (Audience value)
7. [Actionable advice question]
8. [Common mistakes question]
9. [Tool or framework question]

### Closing (Takeaways)
10. [Immediate action listeners can take]
11. [Bold prediction or contrarian take]
12. [Where to learn more]
```

## Example Questions

**Bad:** "Can you tell us about your background in product management?"

**Good:** "You led discovery at [Company] through a 10x scale-up. What's one discovery practice that most teams skip that would actually save them months?"

**Bad:** "What do you think about AI in product development?"

**Good:** "You posted that 'AI won't replace PMs, but PMs who use AI will replace those who don't.' What's one concrete way a PM should be using AI this week that most aren't?"

## Research Sources

**When LinkedIn URL provided:**
- Use browser MCP to visit and read profile directly
- Extract recent posts, activity, and content
- Review experience, skills, and featured sections

**Additional web research:**
- Company website and product
- Conference talks or presentations
- Blog posts or articles
- Podcast appearances
- Twitter/X or other social media
- GitHub (if technical)
- Press coverage or interviews

## Notes

- Research is current as of search date - verify timing-sensitive details
- Focus on publicly available information
- Questions are customized to your Product Builders audience
- Style matches your direct, no-fluff approach
- Prioritize practical value over biography
